Chapter One

The Tale of Arsène & Majorine

A story that began long before they knew it would

Chapter I

The Arrival

The rains had just begun to soften the dust of the new neighborhood when Arsène arrived. The sky hung low and silver, as though it too was adjusting to unfamiliar surroundings. Everything smelled new — wet earth, fresh paint, quiet beginnings.

He was in his final year Form 6, standing on the edge of his UACE exams, his mind often crowded with equations, expectations, and the heavy silence of responsibility. Love, if it existed for him, was something distant — like a song playing faintly from another room.

Until he met Majorine.

Chapter II

The Spark

She entered his life not as a gentle melody, but as a sharp note — unexpected, disruptive. Majorine was in Senior 4, confident, stubborn, and unapologetically expressive. Her laughter could fill a street, and her silence could freeze one.

"You could move, you know," she said, arms crossed, chin lifted.
"I was here first," Arsène replied, equally unmoved.

Their first encounter was far from romantic — just two forces meeting on a narrow path between houses, neither willing to step aside. That was all it took.

Chapter III

The Softening

Time, as it always does, began to soften the sharp edges. Their paths crossed too often to ignore. At the small shops, near the water point, along the dusty roads that connected their homes — there was always that moment of recognition.

Arsène began to notice the small things. The way Majorine tucked her hair behind her ear when she was thinking. The way her eyes softened when she wasn't trying to prove a point. The quiet kindness she showed to younger children in the neighborhood.

And Majorine began to see Arsène differently too. He was patient. He listened more than he spoke. And when he smiled — rare, but genuine — it felt like sunlight breaking through clouds.

Chapter IV

Distance & Devotion

But life, as it often does, demanded change. Majorine returned to school to begin her A-Level journey. The distance was not just physical — it was emotional, temporal. Yet, even in the glow of his screen, in the quiet hum of his thoughts, there was always one constant. Her.

Every Sunday became sacred. It was the one day she could reach him — borrowing a friend's phone, stepping away from the noise of school life to hear his voice.

"Hello?" he would answer, though he already knew it was her.
"Hi…" she would say softly, her usual confidence melting into something tender.

Majorine's sister, Mercy, became an unexpected guardian of their love — creating opportunities for them to meet at quiet restaurants tucked away from the noise, at hidden corners where time seemed to slow.

Chapter V

A Love Like This

They were free with each other in a way that is rare. No masks. No pretenses. Just truth. And though the world around them continued to change — exams, responsibilities, the uncertain future — their love remained constant. Not perfect. Not effortless. But real.

They were something deeper — a partnership, a friendship, a love that felt like a promise not yet spoken but already kept.

They didn't need much. Just each other.

Because sometimes, love doesn't begin with a spark. Sometimes, it begins with resistance — with two people standing on a narrow path, unwilling to move.

Until one day, they realize they don't have to move away. They can walk forward. Together.

"And in that reality, they found something beautiful — a love that grew quietly, like roots beneath the soil: unseen, but strong."
